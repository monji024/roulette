Somewhere, a clock keeps time no one asked it to keep.
You did not come here to win. You came here to find out.
The door behind you no longer feels like an exit.
Six empty chambers. One that is not.
The house does not need to cheat. The odds already work for it.

[SPIN]
The chamber rotates.
Metal whispers against metal.
Something clicks into place, and you are not told what.
The cylinder spins longer than it should.

Randomness has never felt so personal.

[PRE_TRIGGER]
One decision remains.
Your hand does not shake. That will change soon, or it will not matter.
The odds are listening.
Six ways this ends well. One way it does not.
This is the part no one rehearses for.
Somewhere between courage and math, you make your choice.
The terminal waits.

[SURVIVE]
Nothing happens. Everything happens.
The hammer falls on an empty chamber.
You are still here. That is not the same as being safe.
Luck has not abandoned you. Yet.
Your pulse returns first. Your thoughts, a moment later.
Another round begins, whether you are ready or not.
Survival is not a reward. It is a postponement.

[DEATH]

The terminal falls silent.
No further moves remain.

There is no undo for this kind of silence.

[AMBIENT]

A draft moves through a room with no windows.
The lights dim by a fraction no meter could measure.
You hear a second heartbeat that is not yours.
The walls seem closer than the last round.

For a moment, the game seems to be studying you back.

Someone else's decision, someone else's silence.

[VICTORY]

[LAST_CHANCE]

[RULES]
Two players. One revolver. Six chambers. One bullet.

Survive a pull, and the odds — and your score — grow.

[ROOM]

[FAREWELL]

Until next time — if there is one.
